Mobile Radiation Monitoring System

Mobile radiation detection systems are designed to monitor the radiofrequency (RF) radiation emitted by mobile phones and other wireless devices, and alert the user when the levels exceed safe limits. These systems can use built-in sensors, external meters, or apps that estimate exposure based on signal strength. When high radiation is detected, the user receives notifications through pop-ups, sounds, or vibrations, prompting them to take safety measures such as using speaker mode, keeping the phone away from the body, or reducing call duration. Such alerts help raise awareness, encourage safer phone usage, and reduce prolonged exposure to electromagnetic radiation.

Coustomer Churn Detection

Customer churn detection is the process of identifying customers who are likely to stop using a company’s product or service. By analysing historical data such as demographic details, usage behaviour, payment history, complaints and support interactions, organisations can build predictive models to estimate the probability of churn. This allows businesses to take proactive measures like personalised offers, improved service quality or loyalty programmes to retain valuable customers. Common techniques for churn detection include data cleaning, feature engineering, exploratory data analysis and the use of machine learning classification algorithms such as logistic regression, random forests or gradient boosting to accurately predict which customers are at risk of leaving.
